Exploring Mars

How we explore Mars

A lot if different government organisations and companies are currently interested in exploring Mars. All the big nations want to be the first to know the most about our neighbour planet, and companies like SpaceX are currently interested to turn Mars into some commercial use.

Currently, there is only a couple of countries that explore Mars. The leading country is the USA with its organisation Nasa.

Satelites

We are currently still using a lot of different satellites to explore Mars and collect data. They orbit around the Planet, and collect data like images of the surface, weather data and mineral data. They are also used to transfer data from the rivers and other technical devices from mars to earth.

On the right is the Reconnaissance Orbiter, which collects data like minerals, weather, and helps the data transfer from the rovers to earth.

Its in operation since March 10th, 2006 and has transmitted over 400 Terabits of data

Curiosity

Curiosity is one of the oldest Rover from Nasa, and also one of the first rovers on the martian surface.

Opportunity

Perseverance

Perseverance is the newst and currently only function Rover on Mars. It tries to find remains of Life on Mars. It also brings a drone with it, that flies autonomously on Mars.

The Rover is build like its predecessor, Curiosity, and only has technical changes and other equipment changes. The Systems of Curiosity got improved and reused in the Perseverance rover.

The Rover was sent to Mars in 2020, and landed it early 2021, and began its operation successfully.
