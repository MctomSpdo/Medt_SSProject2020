How we explore Mars

A lot of different government organisations and companies are currently interested in exploring Mars. All the big nations want to be the first to know the most about our neighbour planet, and companies like SpaceX are currently interested to turn Mars into some commercial use.

Currently, there is only a couple of countries that explore Mars. The leading country is the US with its organisation Nasa.

Satelites

We are currently still using a lot of different satellites to explore Mars and collect data. They orbit around the Planet, and collect data like images of the surface, weather data and mineral data. They are also used to transfer data from the rivers and other technical devices from mars to earth.

On the right is the Reconnaissance Orbiter, which collects data like minerals, weather, and helps the data transfer from the rovers to earth.

Its in operation since March 10th, 2006 and has transmitted over 400 Terabytes of data

Curiosity

Curiosity is one of the most iconic rovers from Nasa. It started its mission on August 6, 2012. The Rover is still active to this day, as of May 20th, 2021. It has survived over 3000 sols (Martian days) on our neighbour planet.

Due to some problems in a heating in the arm of Curiosity, it often has to enter a deep sleep mode to recharge its batteries, which decreases Efficiency.

Opportunity

Opportunity started its operation in January 2004, and sent its last message on June 10th, 2018. It operated for over 8 martian Years (15 Earth years). The last message was translated by Jacob Margolis, a journalist, and says "My battery is low and it's getting dark" The Rover operated over 5 Years, even tho the initial Plan was just a couple of months. The Rover survived longer than anyone has ever thought.

Since its really cold on Mars, the Rovers have to constantly use energy to heat themselves, otherwise the electronics freeze to death. The Rover died during the biggest dust storm in the mission. The control team sent over 800 commands since then, but never got a response back.

Its twin, Spirit, is built the exact same way, and landed on the planet on the other side a bit later than opportunity

Perseverance

Perseverance is the newest and currently only functioning Rover on Mars. It tries to find remains of Life on Mars. It also brings a drone with it, that flies autonomously on Mars.

The Rover is build like its predecessor, Curiosity, and only has technical changes and other equipment changes. The Systems of Curiosity got improved and reused in the Perseverance rover.

The Rover was sent to Mars in 2020, and landed in early 2021, and began its operation successfully.
